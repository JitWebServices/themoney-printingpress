About Safe Withdrawal Rates

The withdrawal rate determines how much of your retirement corpus you can safely withdraw each year. A lower rate means your money will last longer, but you'll need to save more. The traditional "4% rule" suggests that withdrawing 4% annually from your retirement savings should make your money last 30 years, but more conservative approaches use 2-3%.

Tips for Retirement Planning

  • Start saving early to benefit from compound interest
  • Consider inflation when planning your retirement income needs
  • Diversify your investments to manage risk
  • Review and adjust your plan regularly
  • Account for healthcare costs in retirement
